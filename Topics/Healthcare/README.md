# A02 Recent Advancements and Future Trends in AI

This assignment will explore recent advancements in artificial intelligence using AlphaFold, the protein-folding AI developed by Google DeepMind. It will cover what’s new in AI in this field, future trends, and how these relate to real industries. 
AI developments in recent years illustrate how technology is increasingly able to solve real scientific issues. AlphaFold is an excellent example. The version can now predict not only the 3D shapes of proteins but also how they interact with drugs, DNA, RNA, and other molecules. This makes it much easier to design new medicines. 
In 2025, the free AlphaFold Protein Structure Database now offers over 200 million protein structures, which more than 3 million researchers from over 190 countries use daily. These tools have saved scientists years of lab work, millions of dollars, and inspired new programs like Boltz-2, which estimates drug and protein binding affinity. Overall, the use of AI in laboratories has become not only more accurate but also more productive and practical. 
In the future, tools like AlphaFold could evolve into smart agents that not only predict protein structures but also propose experiments and analyze results automatically. Researchers expect to see full simulations of cell parts and the ability to design completely new proteins from scratch for pharmaceutical or material purposes. AlphaFold went from “interesting” to “we now understand life better than ever. It shows “super-smart” AI in the specific area of biology, but full AGI, artificial general intelligence that matches human thinking in all areas, is still considered to be quite a few years away. AlphaFold is also connected to emerging technologies like space exploration. It acts as a small step toward AGI by solving extremely difficult problems on its own in protein folding. In space exploration, this tool can be used to identify the protein structures of bacteria that are resistant to radiation or extremely cold temperatures, which can lead to better astronaut shielding or stronger materials for space missions. 
Despite its advantages, AlphaFold presents some legal and ethical concerns. The model's predictions may be biased because the AI has been trained mostly with well-studied proteins, so it may perform poorly in some cases. We know that this tool can be used freely for educational purposes, but there are commercial restrictions that mean that big companies benefit more. Some scientists treat the predictions as completely accurate without doing real experiments, which can lead to mistakes. There is also a risk of misuse, such as creating dangerous biological agents, or concerns about privacy if genetic data is handled improperly. 
In the current industry landscape, AlphaFold is already making a big difference. Pharmaceutical companies use it to speed up drug discovery, helping them find new treatments for diseases like cancer more quickly. In biotechnology, it supports the design of enzymes that break down plastic or clean pollution and turn waste into fuel. In Agriculture, proteins help crops survive drought and pests without chemicals. For space missions, it contributes to creating biological materials and health solutions suited to harsh environments. These examples show the impact on medicine, biology, and engineering will keep growing for decades.
The future holds exciting possibilities thanks to AI like AlphaFold: Drugs designed in days, organs grown on demand, microbes that eat plastic and CO₂ for breakfast. AlphaFold is a perfect example of how AI is quickly revolutionizing science. It facilitates practical work and opens the door to talk about new technologies such as AGI and space exploration.
